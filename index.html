Condylar Fracture Surgery

Individualized surgical treatment based on fracture.

Accomplishments
  • Diagnosis and Assessment:Condyle fractures are diagnosed through clinical examination and radiological assessment, with extracapsular condylar fractures being common.
  • Diagnosis and Assessment:Surgical treatment involves internal stabilization using fine screws and plates, and surgery can be delayed if necessary.
  • Surgical Techniques:Various techniques include osteosynthesis, mini-systems with rigid fixation, and pin insertion through cutaneous approaches.
  • Considerations: Individualized management based on fracture type, patient age, and close postoperative care are crucial.
Pediatric Fracture Management

Customized treatment for pediatric fractures.

Accomplishments
  • Diagnosis and Assessment:Pediatric fractures require careful evaluation, considering growth plates and age-specific factors. Radiological assessment helps determine fracture type and alignment.
  • Treatment Options:Non-surgical management (casting, splinting) is preferred for stable fractures. Surgical intervention (internal fixation, external fixation) may be necessary for displaced or unstable fractures.
  • Growth Considerations:Pediatric bones have remarkable healing capacity due to active growth. Fractures near growth plates require precise reduction to avoid growth disturbances.
  • Rehabilitation and Follow-up:Post-fracture rehabilitation focuses on restoring function and preventing stiffness. Regular follow-up ensures proper healing and growth monitoring.
Space Infection Surgery

Diagnosis, antibiotics, drainage, care.

Accomplishments
  • Diagnosis and Assessment:Accurate diagnosis involves clinical examination and imaging (CT, MRI), identifying involved spaces and assessing severity.
  • Antibiotics and Drainage:Broad-spectrum antibiotics target common pathogens, while surgical drainage (I&D) is essential for localized or spreading infections.
  • Airway Considerations:Evaluate airway patency, risk of compromise, and consider tracheostomy or intubation in severe cases.
  • Postoperative Care and Follow-up:Focus on wound care, regular follow-up, and collaboration with specialists as needed.
Maxillary Osteomyelitis

Prompt diagnosis, surgery, and antibiotics are crucial for maxillary osteomyelitis.

Accomplishments
  • Definition and Causes:Infection of the upper jawbone (maxilla) caused by bacteria or fungi spreading from nearby tissues or bloodstream.
  • Symptoms:Fever, severe bone pain, swelling, redness, and discharge near the infected area.
  • Treatment:Surgical intervention (sequestrectomy, debridement), antibiotics, and adjunct therapies.
  • Complications and Prognosis:Early treatment prevents permanent bone loss and tissue damage.
